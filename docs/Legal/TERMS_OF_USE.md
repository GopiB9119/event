# Community Ledger Beta Terms

Effective date: 11 July 2026

Publisher: **Gopi Banoth**, independent publisher of Community Ledger. Private support: [banothgopikrishna19@gmail.com](mailto:banothgopikrishna19@gmail.com).

## 1. Purpose

Community Ledger is a local-first Android tool for recording community event donations, credits, expenses, and debits with reviewed receipt evidence.

## 2. No Money Or Investment Service

Community Ledger does not collect donations, execute payments, hold funds, offer returns, arrange investments, or provide banking, accounting, fundraising, legal, tax, or financial advice.

Never send money solely because an event, member, receipt, or event-copy link appears in the app. Verify the organizer and payment destination independently.

## 3. Human Review Is Required

OCR and parsed receipt details can be incomplete or wrong. Users are responsible for checking names, amounts, dates, payment references, and totals against original records before saving or relying on them.

## 4. Local And Independent Ledgers

Each installation owns an independent local ledger. Event-copy links do not synchronize members, transactions, balances, or receipts. Public/private is a local marker, not access control. Links do not prove identity or authorization.

## 5. Responsible Use

Users must not use the app to:

- create false receipts or misleading financial records
- impersonate an organizer or event
- hide expenses or misrepresent donation totals
- send spam or unwanted invitations
- collect personal information without a lawful reason or required consent
- facilitate fraud, unlawful fundraising, or deceptive investment claims

## 6. Data And Recovery

The beta has no cloud backup, export, restore, or account recovery. Users must keep independent records for important or irreplaceable money information. Uninstalling the app or losing the device deletes the local ledger.

## 7. Beta Reliability

The beta may contain defects, OCR errors, compatibility issues, and incomplete workflows. Test each release before relying on it. Stop using the app when its limitations do not fit the event's needs.

Beta terms

Plain-language conditions for using Community Ledger as a local event record.

Publisher: Community Ledger is independently published by Gopi Banoth. Private support: banothgopikrishna19@gmail.com.

1. Purpose

Community Ledger records community event donations, credits, expenses, and debits with reviewed receipt evidence.

2. No money or investment service

The app does not collect donations, execute payments, hold funds, offer returns, arrange investments, or provide banking, accounting, fundraising, legal, tax, or financial advice.

Never send money solely because an event, member, receipt, or link appears in the app. Verify organizers and payment destinations independently.

3. Human review is required

OCR and parsed receipt details can be incomplete or wrong. Users must check names, amounts, dates, payment references, and totals against original records.

4. Local and independent ledgers

Each installation owns an independent ledger. Event-copy links do not synchronize members, transactions, balances, or receipts. Public/private is a local marker, not access control.

5. Responsible use

Do not use Community Ledger to create false evidence, impersonate organizers, hide expenses, misrepresent donation totals, send spam, collect data without a lawful reason, facilitate fraud, or make deceptive investment claims.

6. Data and recovery

The beta has no cloud backup, export, restore, or account recovery. Keep independent records for important money information. Uninstalling or losing the device deletes its local ledger.

7. Beta reliability

The beta can contain defects, OCR errors, compatibility issues, and incomplete workflows. Test every release and stop using it when the limitations do not fit the event's needs.

8. Support

Email banothgopikrishna19@gmail.com for private support. Use the public project support page only for non-sensitive bugs. Do not post receipts, phone numbers, email addresses, UPI IDs, transaction references, passwords, signing keys, or verification codes there.

9. Changes

Features and beta terms may change. Material changes will be reflected in release notes and the in-app Trust Center.
